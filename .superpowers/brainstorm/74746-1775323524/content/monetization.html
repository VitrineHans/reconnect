How does Reconnect make money?

Users pay nothing — ever. Here are four models that keep it that way.

🎵
Sponsored Question Packs

Brand-Native Content

Brands like Spotify, Netflix, Nike, or Airbnb sponsor themed question packs. "What song are you right now?" powered by Spotify. "If you could go anywhere together?" powered by Airbnb.

Revenue model

Brands pay per pack activation or per completed response

Brand fit

High — lifestyle brands that want to be part of real friendships

User experience

Feels like curated content, not ads. Never interrupts the loop.

🏢
B2B Licensing

The Relationship Engine

License the core technology to businesses. Couples therapists use it with clients. HR teams use it for team culture. Dating apps embed it post-match. Reconnect is free on the surface — B2B pays underneath.

Revenue model

SaaS per-seat or API licensing to partners

Brand fit

Strong — validates the product's depth and seriousness

User experience

Completely invisible to consumer users

🎁
Friendship Gifts

Gift Economy

Users can send real gifts to friends — flowers, coffee, an experience — triggered by in-app moments. Hit a 30-day streak? App suggests "Celebrate with a coffee on us." Reconnect takes a cut of every purchase.

Revenue model

Commission on gift marketplace transactions (10–20%)

Brand fit

Perfect — gifts are the natural expression of a deepened friendship

User experience

Feels celebratory, never forced. Spending is optional and emotionally motivated.

🔬
Insights & Research

Anonymized Data Licensing

Reconnect sits on a gold mine: what questions people engage with, which categories strengthen bonds, how friendships evolve. Sell anonymized trend reports to researchers, publishers, media companies, and brands.

Revenue model

Research partnerships, data licensing, trend reports

Brand fit

Medium — requires strong privacy narrative to maintain trust

User experience

Invisible, but must be clearly disclosed and opt-in to avoid backlash

These can be combined — click all that feel right
